\addtocontents{toc}{\emph{From the cover:} In this issue, we salute Galileo Galilei (1564-1642) who revolutionized science and developed the experimental method. We consider objects falling under the influence of gravity, as well as objects rolling on an inclined plane. We examine the kinematics of motion in these two cases, putting various hypotheses of motion to the test. \emph{In nullius verba,} we will let evidence, and not authority, decide. \emph{Cover image: \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Galileo_Galilei_(1564-1642)_RMG_BHC2700.tiff}{Justus Sustermans, Portrait of Galileo Galilei, circa 1640, oil and wood on canvas, Royal Museums Greenwich.}} \bigskip\\}
